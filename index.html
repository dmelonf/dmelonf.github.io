Astrophysics · Radiative transfer · Planet formation

David Melon Fuksman

Postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (Heidelberg). I build and apply radiation‑hydrodynamics methods (two‑/three‑temperature M1 schemes) to study protoplanetary discs, their instabilities, and the formation of planets.

Research

A quick overview of what I do now and what I’ve done so far. Below are two tracks: Numerical methods (tools I develop) and Science (questions I study).

Numerical methods

M1 radiation hydrodynamics in PLUTO

Two- and three‑temperature RHD modules and tests, aimed at irradiated disc environments and embedded protoplanets.

ApJ (2021) A&A (2024)

Implicit matrix‑free hydro (MATRICS)

An implicit, matrix‑free Eulerian hydrodynamics solver for stiff problems and large timesteps in disc simulations.

A&A (2024)

Monte‑Carlo / post‑processing

Radiative transfer post‑processing of RHD simulations for observable predictions of accreting protoplanets.

A&A (2025)

Science

Vertical shear instability (VSI) & disc structure

Radiation‑hydrodynamical exploration of VSI in starlight‑heated discs; consequences for turbulence and observables.

A&A (2024)

Signatures of embedded protoplanets

Thermal & kinematic signals of accreting protoplanets; gap illumination and spiral excitation.

A&A (2024) A&A (2024)

Disc morphology & observations

From WaOph 6 arms to self‑shadowing tests: connecting RHD models with A&A / ApJ results and ALMA comparisons.

A&A (2021) ApJ (2022)
